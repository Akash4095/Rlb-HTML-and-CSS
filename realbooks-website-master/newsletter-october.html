October, 2020

This month on 1st October 2 major compliances have kicked in – a phase wise implementation of e-invoicing and TCS (tax collected at source) for goods sold. The purpose of e-invoicing is quite clear – more real-time information for the government, all data getting automatically populated in the portals and therefore less scope for any sort of manipulation.

This has started with companies over Rs. 500 crores with Rs. 100 crores expected in the next 3 months and I feel the government will keep reducing the threshold in a phased manner.

The TCS compliance while more cumbersome, gives the Government a small boost in tax collections and now pretty much gets most transactions into the tax net. Earlier, barring certain specified goods, sale of goods did not attract any TDS or TCS.

With this, each buyer is automatically depositing a certain amount of advance tax with the government (if you are purchasing goods worth more than Rs. 50 lakhs from the same supplier).

The RealBooks team has spent a good amount of time ensuring that both these compliance get completed and am happy to say that we have been able to meet the deadlines for both e-invoicing and TCS.

RealBooks continues to innovate and enhance its software and next up we shall be integrating GST return filing via a GSP and also have launched invoicing via our Mobile App.
